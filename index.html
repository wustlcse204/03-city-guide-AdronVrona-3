My Favorite Things to Do in the Louisville, Kentucky

The Louisville Slugger Museum and Factory

The Louisville Slugger Museum and Factory is one of the most iconic attractions in the city of Louisville. It's also very cheap! (see our price table) There are a ton of fun things to do inside the museum, including:

  • A tour of bat models created by some of the greatest baseball players ever
  • "The First Bat" turning demonstration
  • A "Behind the Brand" burning demonstration
  • Mini bat and nub decorating

Churchill Downs

Churchill Downs is a beautiful racetrack and the home of the beloved Kentucky Derby. Here, you can come to get a nice bite to eat, hang out with your friends/family, and watch some intense horse races. Churchill Downs is a popular "going out" location for people of any age in the Louisville area.

UofL or UK Sporting Event

If there's one thing people around the United States know Kentucky for, its the strength of our college teams. UofL's and UK's college basketball and football teams are consisently some of the strongest in the country. The home games that they play here can be absolutely electrifying; you can feel the passion of our fans from any seat in the house. If you're interested in viewing one while you're down in the Commonwealth, here is a schedule of UofL's sporting events.

UK vs UofL Fans

University of Kentucky

UK was founded in 1865 and is centered in Lexington, Kentucky. The school has 29,000 students, and its colors are blue and white. The sports teams at UK are called the Wildcats, and their teams play in the SEC conference. Their stadium is called Kroger Field, and their arena is called Rupp Arena.

University of Louisville

UofL was founded in 1798 and is centered in Louisville, Kentucky. The school has 23,000 students, and its colors are red and black. The sports teams at UofL are called the Cardinals, and their teams play in the ACC conference. Their stadium is called Cardinal Stadium, and their arena is called the KFC Yum! Center

Bourbon Distillery Tours

If you didn't know it already, Kentucky is a state full of bourbon lovers. Kentucky is considered the birthplace of bourbon, and we currently have been responsible for over 95% of the world's current supply. It is no surprise, then, that Kentucky houses some of the world's most elite and historic distilleries right here in our state. Here, you can engage in delicious bourbon taste tests and gain an understanding behind the beginnings of bourbon production in the US.
